Detection of Hate and Offensive Speech on Social Networks

Presentation
Challenge The rise of social media has created new opportunities for communication and connection. However, it has also led to the proliferation of hate speech and offensive language, which can harm individuals and communities. The challenge was to build an effective and scalable solution that could detect explicit and implicit hate speech, helping social platforms moderate content and ensure user safety.
Results We developed TextShield, a robust Natural Language Processing (NLP)-based system using RoBERTa that achieved:
  • 92.45% accuracy on the validation set and 90.70% accuracy on the test set.
  • Detection of subtle and implicit forms of offensive language through contextual understanding.
  • Deployment of a user-friendly web app to demo the solution, enabling easy adoption by social media platforms.

Discovery and Ideation

Identify the Problem:

Social networks struggle to moderate hate and offensive speech, particularly implicit forms that are context-dependent.

Research:

Analyzed existing datasets, social media policies, and challenges faced by platforms in moderating speech.

Brainstorm Ideas:

Explored machine learning-based approaches, focusing on contextual NLP models.

Defining the Product Vision

Create a Product Vision:

Develop an AI solution capable of moderating offensive content while minimizing false positives and negatives.

Define the Product Roadmap:

We defined a roadmap that prioritized quick wins and high-impact changes. The core milestones included:

  • Data collection and preprocessing.
  • Model experimentation.
  • Rigorous testing with data from different social media platforms.
  • Build and deploy a web app
  • Launch, presentation and demo of our product.
