Presentation
Challenge The rapid growth of tourism highlighted the inefficiencies in managing tours manually. Tourists often have to use multiple applications to plan their tripsâ€”finalizing places to visit, searching for accommodations, identifying travel routes, purchasing tickets, and managing itineraries. This process is cumbersome, time-consuming, and prone to errors, leading to a less enjoyable experience for travelers.
Results TourWizard simplifies and centralizes the tour planning process. It provides a seamless platform where users can search for cities, accommodations, and travel bookings, and generate a comprehensive itinerary. The system ensures a more efficient and enjoyable experience for tourists while reducing the complexity of tour management.
My Role For this project, I took up multiple roles to create a successful product:
  • As a Product Manager my responsibilities were:
    • Defining the product vision and scope
    • Conducting research on user needs and accessibility challenges
    • Coordinating cross-functional collaboration with designers, researchers, and developers
    • Presenting a live demo of our product
  • As a Project Manager my responsibilities were:
    • Lead the end-to-end development and deployment of the product
    • Organize sprints
    • Facilitate daily stand-ups and weekly reports
    • Manage the project backlog
  • As a Developer my responsibilities were:
    • Design and plan the UI/UX of the webapp
    • Developed the front-end
    • Designed and maintained the back-end database
Key Features
  • Browse without logging in: Users can explore cities, accommodations, and flights without creating an account.
  • User authentication: Required for booking, saving places, or creating itineraries, with Google and Twitter login options.
  • Search functionality: Enables users to find cities, accommodations, and flights.
  • Itinerary management: Automatically generates itineraries based on user selections.
  • Seamless booking: Allows users to finalize tours, book services, and make payments directly through the platform.

Discovery and Ideation

Identify the Problem:

Manual tour planning was inefficient, requiring multiple applications and extensive effort.

Research:

Conducted surveys and user interviews to understand tourist pain points and preferences. Analyzed competitive platforms to identify gaps in the market.

Brainstorm Ideas:

Ideated a centralized platform to streamline tour planning, incorporating features such as:

  • Search
  • Booking
  • Payment
  • Itinerary generation

Defining the Product Vision

Create a Product Vision:

Build a unified platform offering effortless tour management with an intuitive interface.

Define the Product Roadmap:

We defined a roadmap that prioritized quick wins and high-impact changes. The core milestones included:

  • Initial user research and problem discovery
  • Adding user authentication
  • Incorporating 3rd party APIs for search functionalityâ€™s
  • Itinerary generation
  • Payment integration

Prioritization and Backlog Management

Prioritize Features:

Using the MoSCoW method, we focused on Must-Have features for our Minimum Viable Product (MVP):

  • Must Have: Essential functionalities like search, booking, and itinerary generation
  • Should Have: Payment
  • Could Have: Advanced features like collaborative planning

Manage the Product Backlog:

Maintained a prioritized backlog of tasks to ensure a streamlined development process We created this backlog of features prioritized based on feasibility, user impact, and alignment with our goals.

Design and Development

Collaborate with Design Team:

Working closely with our designers, we created wireframes for the main features. The design focused on:

  • Landing Page
  • Sign up and Log In Pages
  • Explore Cities Page
  • Explore Hotels Page
  • Search Flights Page
  • My Itinerary Page

Define Specifications:

We defined clear specifications for our developers, and clear technical requirements for developers to implement features like search, booking, and itinerary generation.

Oversee Development:

We worked iteratively in sprints, ensuring that each feature aligned with the roadmap and addressed user pain points. Ensured the website adhered to the product vision and resolved technical challenges promptly.

Testing and Quality Assurance

Conduct Testing:

Performed unit testing, integration testing, and end-to-end testing to ensure the platform's reliability.

Gather Feedback:

Collected input from test users to refine the design and address usability concerns.

Launch and Post-Launch

Prepare for Launch:

Deployed the website and ensured the platform was ready for public use.

Monitor Performance:

Post-launch, we suggested tracking key metrics such as:

  • Reduction in planning time
  • Increase in User engagement
  • Customer Satisfaction

Iterate and Improve:

Our roadmap included future iterations based on ongoing user feedback. Implemented enhancements based on user feedback, such as advanced filtering for flights and hotels. The flexibility of the design allowed for incremental improvements as more data on user behavior became available.
