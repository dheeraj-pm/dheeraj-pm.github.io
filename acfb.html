Atlanta Community Food Bank

Combating Food Insecurity - A Morgan Stanley CodeToGive Hackathon project

Role Product Manager
Client Atlanta Community Food Bank

Presentation/Full-Documentation
Challenge The Atlanta Community Food Bank faced several challenges in ensuring frequent, consistent, and convenient delivery of equitable food to partners. Partners needed to order a minimum of 6,000 pounds of food for delivery, leading to missed deliveries or additional costs for renting vehicles. Additionally, there was a lack of consistent tools, information, and standard practices among partners. Furthermore, many community members had limited English proficiency, making it difficult for them to utilize the food provided effectively.
Results Our solution addressed the Atlanta Community Food Bank’s challenges through an innovative approach that improved delivery efficiency, standardized practices, and enhanced accessibility for non-English speakers. We created a clustering algorithm that optimizes delivery routes by grouping partners based on order size and proximity. A learning center provided consistent tools and information for partners, and a multilanguage support feature enhanced accessibility for the diverse community.
My Role As a Product Manager in this project, I led the team through the ideation, design, and development phases. My responsibilities included:
  • Defining the product vision and scope
  • Creating the product roadmap
  • Prioritizing features for the MVP
  • Overseeing collaboration between the development and design teams
  • Contributing to the design and development process
  • Presenting the final solution to the Morgan Stanley and ACFB stakeholders
I ensured alignment with business and community needs while maintaining a focus on scalability and sustainability.

Discovery and Ideation

Identify the Problem:

Partners had to meet a high minimum order size for food deliveries, and inconsistent tools and language barriers made it challenging to leverage the food provided.

Research:

Conducted market research on food distribution challenges and gathered feedback from community members through surveys and interviews.

Brainstorm Ideas:

Explored various solutions, including clustering algorithms for optimizing delivery and creating multilingual support systems.

Defining the Product Vision

Create a Product Vision:

Aimed to develop an accessible, efficient, and scalable solution that would streamline food delivery and support underserved communities.

Define the Product Roadmap:

We defined a roadmap that prioritized quick wins and high-impact changes. The core milestones included:

  • Clustering algorithm for delivery optimization
  • A learning center for consistent processes
  • User testing with color-blind individuals
  • Multilingual features for inclusivity

Prioritization and Backlog Management

Prioritize Features:

Used the MoSCoW method to prioritize the clustering algorithm as a must-have, the learning center as a should-have, and multilingual support as a could-have feature.

Manage the Product Backlog:

Maintained a detailed backlog of features, user stories, and enhancements, ensuring alignment with project goals.

Design and Development

Collaborate with Design Team:

Working closely with our designers, we created wireframes for the new features. The design focused on:

  • Prototype for the learning center
  • Prototype for multilingual support system
  • Design of the optimized route page

Define Specifications:

Provided clear requirements and technical specifications for the clustering algorithm and UI elements.

Oversee Development:

Guided the development process, ensuring that the product aligned with our vision and that key milestones were met.

Testing and Quality Assurance

Conduct Testing:

Performed functional testing of the clustering algorithm to ensure accuracy in delivery route optimization. Tested multilingual support features with different language settings.

Gather Feedback:

Collected feedback from partners and community members through usability testing to refine the learning center and messaging features.

Launch and Post-Launch

Prepare for Launch:

Coordinated with stakeholders for a smooth launch, ensuring the availability of tutorials and support for partners.

Monitor Performance:

Monitored key performance indicators such as reduced delivery failures and increased community engagement

Iterate and Improve:

Collected post-launch feedback to refine the clustering algorithm and enhance multilingual support based on community needs.

Mission to end Hunger
Key Responsibilities Throughout the Process:
  • Customer Focus: Ensured that the solution addressed the needs of both the food bank partners and the community.
  • Strategic Thinking: Aligned the product with the food bank’s mission of equity and inclusivity.
  • Collaboration: Worked closely with cross-functional teams throughout the ideation, development, and testing phases.
  • Data-Driven Decision-Making: Used quantitative metrics like delivery frequency and partner engagement to guide product iterations.
Additional Considerations:
  • Agile Methodologies: Followed Scrum principles to ensure flexibility and adaptability throughout development.
  • Product-Market Fit: Validated that the solution met both the community's needs and the food bank’s operational goals.
  • Continuous Improvement: Set up a feedback loop to continuously improve the system based on user data and feedback.
